# Tax Delinquent / Curative Title Cold Email Campaign — Professional Service Style

## Campaign name

`Email | Tax + Title Friction | Property Situation Review | Texas | 2026-05`

## Campaign purpose

This campaign is for owners or likely rights-holders where Ares has a verified or strongly suspected property-friction signal: tax delinquency, title confusion, heirship/estate chain, old owner/decedent names, liens, vacant property, or land-record anomalies.

Cold email is not where we say “you are delinquent” or “your title is messy.” That creates shame, fear, and legal risk. The email sells a low-pressure **Property Situation Review**: a practical read on whether there is an as-is path before the owner spends time or money fixing, listing, clearing, or untangling everything first.

## Core offer

## The Property Situation Relief Plan

A simple, no-pressure way to find out whether a property with tax, title, ownership, repair, or cleanout friction may have a practical as-is path — without the owner having to repair it, list it, clean it out, or solve every paperwork question before the first conversation.

## Seller-facing promise

> I can give you a quick read on whether an as-is option is even worth discussing for the property — before you spend time or money trying to repair, clean, list, or sort out every issue first.

## Internal targeting signals

Use internally only. Do not weaponize these in copy:

- verified delinquent tax signal
- tax suit / attorney clue
- estate/decedent owner name
- affidavit of heirship / small estate / probate instrument
- old deed chain / partial interest / missing heir clue
- vacant/absentee property
- repairs/code/lien clue
- owner mailing address mismatch
- property likely not retail-ready

## Offer code to infuse everywhere

- without calling out the tax issue in the first touch
- without shaming the owner
- without asking them to solve title first
- without requiring repairs or cleanout first
- without public listing/showings
- before spending time or money on a path they may not want
- quick property read
- practical as-is path
- if it is not a fit, no problem
- if I have the wrong person, I’ll close my notes

## Unique mechanism

# The As-Is Property Situation Review

Instead of asking the owner to fix the property, clean it out, list it, or resolve every paperwork/tax/title question first, Martin reviews the property situation as it sits and determines whether a practical as-is option is worth discussing.

The mechanism is not “we buy tax delinquent houses.”

The mechanism is:

> Start with the property as it sits, review the obvious friction, and decide whether an as-is path is worth a conversation before the owner spends time, money, or emotional energy trying to make the property retail-ready.

## Segment structure

### HOT tax/title friction

Use when Ares has verified delinquency, strong title/estate friction, land-record evidence, or operator-reviewed confidence.

Angle: the property may have become harder to deal with than expected.

### WARM tax/title friction

Use when there are soft signals but not enough proof to reference specifics.

Angle: if repairs, paperwork, taxes, ownership questions, or timing ever become a reason to avoid listing, an as-is path may be worth understanding.

### COLD / ambiguous

Use when contact/property match is weaker.

Angle: wrong-person-safe property note.
