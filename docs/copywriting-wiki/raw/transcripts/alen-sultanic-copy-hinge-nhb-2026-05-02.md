## Key source text / takeaways

Alen describes the **Copy Hinge** as a structural copy change that can materially increase conversion by increasing consumption.

Core claims from the post:

- The Copy Hinge is not a headline change, not a new big idea, and not a mechanism change.
- It is a structural hierarchy change in copy.
- A copy hinge is the section that the rest of the copy revolves around.
- Where the hinge appears depends on market sophistication/awareness.
- For lower sophistication markets, the hinge can live deeper in the copy.
- For higher sophistication/awareness markets, the hinge must move up front.
- If the market is sophisticated and the hinge is buried 5-10 minutes into a VSL/TSL, bounce rates spike.
- The hinge answers the most important early question in the sales process:
  - “What are you giving me in exchange for 5-10 minutes of my time?”
- The reader/viewer pays first with attention and time before money.
- People will usually only agree to give 5-10 minutes of attention at first.
- If the copy convinces them to stay through that first window, conversion probability rises.
- The hinge should tell them:
  - how this is different
  - what is new
  - what they get by staying and consuming the rest of the sales process
- Conversion increases by increasing consumption.
- The higher the consumption, the higher the conversion rate.
- The hinge can work across book-a-call, webinar, VSL, TSL, low-ticket, and ad formats.

Other tactical claims in the post:

- Using “install” instead of learn/get/discover increased conversions 10-30%.
- Using “duplicate” instead of copy increased conversions 64% and doubled qualified leads.
- Adding “in 2024” to headlines doubled conversions on some offers.
- “Show, then tell” with images can increase conversions significantly.
- Short-term thinking language can increase conversions and reduce CPA.
