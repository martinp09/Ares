## Formula

**Recency + Relevance + Personalization = Response in email**

## Recency

What is going on in their environment? What are they aware of that happened recently to their business or environment that they can recall and remember?

Alen's point: response rate has a lot to do with recall and memory. If you can refresh what happened recently, you can rejuvenate response/conversion.

## Relevance

The recent event must be relevant to their desired outcome, professional or otherwise.

Key principle:

- Sell the outcome, not the product.
- Talk about what it does, not what it is.
- Use features/benefits only to support the outcome.
- If you sell to the outcome, the product can sell itself.

## Personalization

Personalize to the person and their stage of the journey toward the outcome they want.

Alen frames the marketer/leader as someone who can “see in the dark” — a few steps ahead of the prospect. The prospect fears the unknown; trust comes from the guide's ability to see the path.

## Email role

Unless the market is product hyper-aware, do not sell the product directly in the email. Focus on the mechanism/outcome. The sales page/VSL sells the product; the email sells the mechanism/outcome and increases intent.

## CTA rule

The CTA should give them something instead of ask for something.
