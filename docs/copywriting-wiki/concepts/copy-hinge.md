# Copy Hinge

The **Copy Hinge** is Alen Sultanic's structural copy principle: the copy needs an early section that answers why the reader should pay with the next 5-10 minutes of attention.

It is not mainly a better headline, hook, story, or new mechanism. It is the section the rest of the copy revolves around.

## Core question

The hinge answers:

> What are you giving me in exchange for 5-10 minutes of my time?

For Ares, this means a seller should not have to read several paragraphs before understanding:

- what Martin is offering
- why it is different from a generic cash-buyer pitch
- what they get by continuing
- why the next step is low-effort and relevant

## Sophistication rule

- Low awareness / low sophistication: the hinge can appear deeper.
- High awareness / high sophistication: move the hinge up front.

Real-estate sellers, especially probate/heir/property owners receiving investor outreach, are likely in a **high-skepticism / high-noise** environment even if they are not product-aware. That means Ares should put the hinge early.

## Ares hinge formula

For seller outreach:

1. Name the situation.
2. Name the offer early.
3. Explain what they get by reading/talking for the next few minutes.
4. Explain how it is different from the default alternatives.
5. Show the low-effort next step.

## Ares probate hinge draft

> If the inherited property is becoming one more project — repairs, cleanup, taxes, title questions, or deciding what everyone wants to do — this note is simply to show you a low-pressure as-is option. In the next few minutes, you can decide whether it is worth a quick call or whether I should close my notes and leave you alone.

## How this changes the offer-first workflow

The old sequence was:

1. Hook
2. Pain
3. Story
4. Offer somewhere later

Ares should use:

1. Situation / relevance
2. Copy hinge: what you get for your attention
3. Pain/friction
4. Mechanism
5. Offer stack
6. CTA
