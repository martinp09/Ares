# Texas Probate Seller Psychology

## HOT

Likely state: urgency, paperwork fatigue, property/tax/title pressure, possible family conflict, higher likelihood of needing a practical next step.

Copy stance: be specific and useful. Acknowledge repairs, taxes, title questions, cleanout, multiple heirs. Offer a simple first conversation, not a guaranteed solution.

## WARM

Likely state: uncertain, maybe not urgent, may be evaluating keep/sell/rent.

Copy stance: softer and less assumptive. Ask whether they are handling the property. Offer to review if selling as-is is worth considering.

## COLD

Likely state: weak signal, may not be the right person, may not perceive problem.

Copy stance: low pressure, wrong-person escape hatch, minimal follow-up unless engagement/confidence improves.

## Phrases to avoid

- “We can solve probate.”
- “Guaranteed close.”
- “No risk.”
- “You must act now.”
- Threatening tax/legal language.
- Anything implying legal/tax advice.
