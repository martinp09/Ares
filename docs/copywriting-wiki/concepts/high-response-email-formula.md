# High Response Email Formula

Alen Sultanic's email formula:

**Recency + Relevance + Personalization = Response**

For Ares, this becomes the email layer that sits after the [[inherited-property-offer-architecture]] and [[copy-hinge]].

## 1. Recency

Tie the email to something recently knowable in the seller's environment.

For Harris probate:

- a recent probate filing
- recent inherited-property decision point
- a recent tax/property signal if verified
- recent mailing/contact attempt
- recent property-status change if known

Ares warning: recency must be real. Do not invent a recent event.

## 2. Relevance

Connect the recent event to the seller's outcome, not Martin's product.

Do not lead with:

> I buy houses cash.

Lead with the outcome/mechanism:

> You may be able to find out whether an as-is option makes sense before the family repairs, cleans out, lists, or solves every issue first.

## 3. Personalization

Personalize to their stage of the journey.

- HOT: likely already feeling pressure; use more concrete property/tax/title/cleanup language.
- WARM: likely evaluating; use softer keep/sell/rent/understand-options language.
- COLD: likely low awareness or wrong person; use right-person and easy opt-out language.

## Email job

Email should sell the **mechanism/outcome**, not the product.

The email's job is not to force a sale. It should increase intent by making the seller want the next step.

## CTA rule

The CTA should give them something instead of asking for something.

Better:

> I can tell you whether an as-is option is even worth discussing.

Weaker:

> Can you call me?

## Ares email checklist

Every serious Ares email should include:

- recency signal
- relevance to seller outcome
- personalization by segment/stage
- mechanism/outcome, not product pitch
- giving CTA
- truth/risk note
